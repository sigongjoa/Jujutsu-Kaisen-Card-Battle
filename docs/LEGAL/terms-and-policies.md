## PART 3: TOURNAMENT RULES AND REGULATIONS (토너먼트 규칙)

### 3.1 Tournament Framework

**Tournament Types**:
- **Casual**: Informal games, house rules allowed
- **Regular**: Standard rules, prize eligible
- **Competitive** (Grand Prix): Official rules strictly enforced
- **Professional** (National/World): Highest standards, strict enforcement

### 3.2 Player Eligibility

**Registration Requirements**:
- Valid account with verified identity
- Payment of entry fee (if applicable)
- Acceptance of tournament rules
- Agreement to Code of Conduct

**Age and Eligibility**:
- **Open Division**: 18+ or parental consent if under 18
- **Junior Division**: Under 13
- **Youth Division**: 13-17
- **Senior Division**: 60+

**Restrictions**:
- Players under 13 must have on-site guardian
- Previous DQ/bans may prevent participation
- Regional tournaments may have residency requirements

### 3.3 Deck Construction Rules

**Deck Size**:
- Minimum: 40 cards
- Maximum: 50 cards
- Decks with <40 cards invalid

**Card Restrictions**:
- **Card Limit**: Maximum 3 copies of any unique card
- **Exception**: Basic lands unlimited copies (if applicable)
- **Banned List**: Certain cards forbidden (updated quarterly)
- **Restricted List**: Some cards limited to 1 copy

**Deck Building Restrictions by Format**:
| Format | Min Cards | Max Copies | Special Rules |
|--------|-----------|-----------|--------------|
| Standard | 40 | 3 | Only current set + 1 prior |
| Extended | 40 | 3 | Last 5 sets legal |
| Legacy | 40 | 3 | All cards legal except banned |

**Current Banned List** (Updated Quarterly):
- [To be populated with current bans]
- Published 1 month before format change effective date
- Provides refund/store credit for newly banned cards

**Deck Check Procedures**:
- Head Judge may request deck inspection anytime
- Verify all cards legal for format
- Check for playset violations
- Cards miscounted/absent penalized

### 3.4 Tournament Formats

**Swiss System** (Most Common):
- All players compete in all rounds
- Pairings based on match records
- No elimination
- 3-5 rounds typical (based on participation)
- Top 8 cut-off after Swiss (64+ players)

**Single Elimination**:
- Lose once and you're out
- Bracket seeding by ranking
- Typical for smaller tournaments (<32 players)

**Round-Robin**:
- Each player plays each other player
- Time-intensive (5+ players = many rounds)
- Used for small groups only

**Sealed Deck**:
- Players provided random card pool
- Build deck from pool only
- Tests card evaluation skills
- Cards shuffled back after tournament

### 3.5 Match Structure

**Best-of-Format**:
- **Best-of-1**: Single game (casual only)
- **Best-of-3**: First to 2 wins (standard)
- **Best-of-5**: First to 3 wins (finals only)

**Time Limits**:
- **Per Game**: 25 minutes (rounds 1-5)
- **Finals**: 50 minutes (best-of-5)
- **Turns**: No individual turn time limit
- **Time Warnings**: 5 minutes, 1 minute before limit

**Actions After Time**:
- Game continues until time called
- Player on turn finishes turn
- Opponent gets one final turn
- Whoever has opponent at 0 HP wins
- If both players have HP >0 at time end: **Draw**

### 3.6 Play Rules and Enforcement

**Game Rules**:
- Comprehensive Rules document governs play
- Tournament rules take precedence over casual
- Judge rulings are final on rules questions

**Rule Enforcement Levels** (REL):

**Regular REL** (Most tournaments):
- Minor infractions: Verbal warning
- Players expected to help catch mistakes
- Judge assists with rule questions

**Competitive REL** (Grand Prix, Nationals):
- Stricter penalty structure
- Players responsible for knowing rules
- Mistakes cannot be rewound

**Professional REL** (Worlds, Championships):
- Strictest enforcement
- No exceptions for player mistakes
- Expert knowledge expected

**Penalty Structure**:
| Infraction | Regular REL | Competitive REL | Professional REL |
|------------|-----------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Game Rule Violation | Warning | Warning | 2 Game Loss Penalty |
| Failure to Maintain | Warning | Warning | 1 Game Loss Penalty |
| Marked Cards | Warning | Warning | 1 Game Loss Penalty |
| Deck List Error | Game Loss | Game Loss | Match Loss |
| Cheating (Intent) | Match Loss | Disqualification | Disqualification + Ban |

### 3.7 Banned Player Conduct

**Disqualification Offenses** (Immediate DQ):
- Cheating (card marking, collusion, deck manipulation)
- Harassment or threats
- Sexual or discriminatory comments
- Intoxication (alcohol or drugs)
- Weapons or violence

**Match Loss Offenses**:
- Failure to follow Head Judge instructions
- Excessive disputes with opponent
- Time stalling
- Physical aggression (minor)

**Warnings and Warnings-Based Bans**:
- Unsporting conduct: 1st warning
- Minor procedural errors: 1st warning
- 3 warnings in single tournament: DQ

### 3.8 Appeal and Dispute Resolution

**Judge Appeals**:
- Player disagrees with judge ruling
- Request Head Judge for second opinion
- Head Judge decision final
- No further appeals

**Tournament Appeals**:
- Serious judgment errors (rare)

- Decision within 7 days

### 3.9 Tournament Integrity

**Anti-Cheating Measures**:
- Random deck checks (10% of players per round)
- Marked card inspections possible
- Extensive replay review in competitive play
- Post-tournament investigations (7-day window)

**Doping-Style Rules** (if applicable):
- No performance-enhancing drugs
- No unauthorized medical assistance
- No use of electronic communication during play
- Players in secured area during matches

**Collusion Detection**:
- Unusual play patterns analyzed
- Multiple players from same group monitored
- Match-fixing (pre-arranged results) forbidden
- Evidence of collusion: Disqualification + ban
